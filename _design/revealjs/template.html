Common Mistakes

Avoiding Careless Errors

Incorrect Approach

Rushing through the problem without reading all answer choices.

"I picked the first answer that seemed right and moved on."

Correct Approach

Reading all options before selecting, even if one looks obviously correct.

"I always check every choice because trap answers exist."

Example 1

Working with Fractions

Problem

Simplify: $\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4}$

Solution

Find common denominator:

$$\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{8}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{11}{12}$$

Answer: $\frac{11}{12}$

Strategy

The "So What?" Test

After every paragraph, ask yourself: "So what?" If the reader can't see why this matters for your MBA goals, revise or cut it.

Example

"I managed a team of 12 engineers" becomes "Managing 12 engineers across 3 time zones taught me that clear async communication matters more than perfect plans."
